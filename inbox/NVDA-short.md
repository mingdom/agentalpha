# NVIDIA (NVDA) – SHORT (tactical into print) – 12–18 months

**Fair Value:** $147 (probability-weighted)
**Current Price:** ~$187 (as of November 17, 2025)
**Downside:** ~21%
**Conviction:** Moderate

---

## 1. Thesis & Expectations Gap

NVIDIA enters its **Q3 FY26 earnings report** (due Wednesday, November 19, 2025, after market close) with **exceptionally high expectations** following nine consecutive quarters of more than 50% year-over-year revenue growth and a blockbuster Blackwell launch. Consensus expectations for FY26 revenue are around **$205–208 billion**, with Q3 estimates near **$46–47 billion** and non-GAAP EPS around **$1.25** (post-split). Buy-side whispers are modestly higher on both revenue and guidance.

At a share price of **$187**, NVIDIA’s market capitalization stands around **$4.53 trillion** (based on ~24.3 billion shares). Backing out **$56.8 billion in net cash**, the current valuation implies approximately **$194 billion of steady-state free cash flow (FCF)** assuming an **8.5% WACC** and **4% terminal growth rate**. By comparison, NVIDIA’s FY25 FCF was **$60.9 billion**, and FY26 run-rate FCF is likely in the **$60–70 billion** range. The bar for incremental upside is therefore extraordinarily high.

**Market Expectation:**
At roughly **41x forward FY26 EPS ($4.56)**, the market is effectively underwriting sustained FCF growth well above any reasonable base rate — essentially pricing in over **$180 billion of FCF** within a few years, built on the assumptions of 30%+ sustained data center growth, mid-70s gross margins, and minimal competitive erosion.

**Our Variant View:**
We believe the market **overestimates the durability and magnitude** of hyperscaler capex growth, while **underestimating** (i) supply and geopolitical constraints (notably around China), (ii) the competitive responses from AMD, Huawei Ascend, and custom silicon (TPUs, AWS Trainium, etc.), and (iii) the rising costs to maintain GPU and networking leadership through the Blackwell Ultra and Rubin generations.

Our forecast assumes FY26 revenue of **$205 billion** (in line with consensus) and FY27 revenue of **$255 billion** (+24% year-over-year vs. buy-side >30%). We also assume **FCF margins normalize to 33–35%** (versus >35–40% implied by the market). While a Q3 beat is possible, **Q4 guidance and data center gross margin trends** will determine whether the narrative holds.

Our **DCF-based fair value** centers around $147 per share, implying roughly 21% downside from current levels. The key expectations gap is between **our modeled steady-state FCF (~$120–130 billion)** and the market’s implied **$190+ billion**.

## 2. Source of Edge (BAIT Framework)

**Primary Edge: Analytical.**
Our edge stems from decomposing the valuation into price-implied FCF durability and benchmarking against historical persistence of supernormal ROIC businesses. Research by Michael Mauboussin and Counterpoint Global indicates that median U.S. ROIC is around **9–10%**, and that extremely high-ROIC outliers rarely sustain their returns for longer than a few years.

The market’s implicit assumption that NVIDIA can **permanently compound FCF at 35–40% margins** at trillion-dollar scale is statistically improbable. Investors are extrapolating 2023–25’s AI infrastructure boom linearly, ignoring natural capex cyclicality, the competitive response, and the limits of data center demand elasticity.

**Who’s on the other side?**
Momentum-driven buyers and passive index allocators who are extrapolating recent hyperscaler spending and assuming NVIDIA’s economics remain monopolistic. Evidence includes serial estimate raises into earnings and growing sell-side narratives pointing to **$1 trillion revenue potential** within the decade.

   * Market assumption: **Smooth restart post-licensing approval**

## Bottom Line

This is **not a call on NVIDIA’s long-term franchise quality** — it remains one of the best businesses in modern history. The call is **tactical**, focused on the **expectations gap** going into an earnings event where the market has priced perfection.

If NVIDIA clears that high bar — with Q4 guidance north of $55 billion, sustained gross margins, and strong commentary on Blackwell supply — the short thesis breaks. If guidance softens or margin compression appears, multiple de-rating toward our **$147 fair value** is likely.
