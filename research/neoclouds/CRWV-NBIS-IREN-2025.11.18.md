Part 1: Nebius Group (NBIS) - The Hypergrowth Mispricing

Fair Value: $154 (prob-weighted) | Current: $92.36 | Upside: +67% | Conviction: High

1. Thesis & Expectations Gap (NBIS)

Nebius Group is a classic "emergence" mispricing driven by investor skepticism regarding execution speed. The company, post-divestiture of its complex legacy Russian assets, is a pure-play AI infrastructure provider with a clean balance sheet and a massive, pre-sold backlog (including multi-billion dollar deals with Microsoft and Meta). The market currently anchors on the low trailing revenue base ($146M Q3 revenue) and recent losses, failing to model the massive, front-loaded capital expenditure (CapEx) as value-accretive infrastructure buildout rather than structural burn.

Market Expectation: At $92.36/share ($23B Market Cap, 74.3x P/E, highly distorted), the market prices in:

Skepticism regarding the management's ambitious guidance of $7-9B annualized revenue by year-end 2026.

A belief that NBIS will struggle to deploy power capacity (MW) and hardware (GPUs) fast enough to meet hyperscaler demand, leading to perpetual delays.

Continued negative sentiment related to capital expenditure, viewing high CapEx as a cash sink.

Our Variant View: The market drastically underestimates the conversion speed of backlog to revenue. Our analysis of CapEx timing and deployment suggests a rapid shift from cash consumption to massive FCF generation. We forecast FY26 revenue of $4.5B (vs low consensus) with a run-rate exceeding $7.5B exiting the year, implying the stock trades at only ~2.8x FY26 run-rate sales. This deep valuation disconnect provides the foundation for our high-conviction call.

2. Source of Edge (BAIT) (NBIS)

Primary Edge: Informational & Analytical

Investors are suffering from anchoring bias related to the firm's complex, risky legacy structure, which has been removed. The analytical edge lies in dissecting the J-Curve of AI infrastructure financing. Current net losses ($100M in Q3) are a function of front-loaded CapEx (building and buying) before the clusters are fully online and generating rent (revenue). Our unit economics model for their specialized GPU clusters implies a low risk of structural unprofitability, with strong cash-on-cash returns once deployed. The balance sheet is strong post-divestiture, providing essential capital access for this massive buildout phase.

3. Linchpins (2-3 Key Drivers) (NBIS)

Linchpin 1: Power Capacity Deployment (MW)

Our view: NBIS successfully ramps active power to 1.5 GW contracted power by end of 2026 to support $7.5B+ ARR exit rate.

Market view: Expects delays, with final capacity deployment falling 30%+ short of company targets.

Rationale: The company is using "pod" and pre-fab data center architectures, leveraging its deep engineering heritage to accelerate build times.

Linchpin 2: Backlog Conversion Rate

Our view: The $20B+ contract pipeline (Microsoft, Meta) converts into firm, recurring revenue with minimal churn or re-negotiation.

Market view: High customer concentration is a risk, and the contracts are viewed as less-firm options.

Rationale: In the GPU arms race, securing capacity is paramount. These contracts act as credit enhancement, allowing NBIS cheaper capital access to fuel the build-out.

Part 2: CoreWeave (CRWV) - The Fallen Angel

Fair Value: $98 (prob-weighted) | Current: $77.14 | Upside: +27% | Conviction: Moderate

1. Thesis & Expectations Gap (CRWV)

CoreWeave is an indispensable AI infrastructure leader, but the stock is currently in the "penalty box" following recent quarterly results that showed operating margin compression (20% to 4%) and concerns over its substantial debt load (over $14B). The stock has retraced significantly from its highs. We view this as a mean-reversion trade driven by an easily identifiable behavioral bias: investors are extrapolating a temporary trough in profitability caused by the heavy infrastructure CapEx required to onboard their $30B+ contracted backlog.

Market Expectation: At $77.14/share ($38.5B Market Cap, negative P/E), the market prices in:

Structural Margin Erosion: A belief that competitive pressure from Big Tech (AWS, Azure) will permanently restrict CoreWeave's operating margins to single digits.

Liquidity/Debt Risk: High leverage ($14B debt and $20B+ CapEx commitment) is seen as unsustainable if the growth rate moderates or delays occur.

Our Variant View: The margin drop is temporary and a classic J-Curve effect in infrastructure buildout, where costs are recognized before revenue. We expect operating margins to snap back to 15-20% within 4-6 quarters as utilization rates on newly installed capacity normalize. Furthermore, contracted revenue backlog ($30B+) provides high visibility, ensuring the debt financing is fundamentally sound. The long-term contracted nature of the business protects against the perceived liquidity crunch.

2. Source of Edge (BAIT) (CRWV)

Primary Edge: Behavioral (Recency Bias)

The investment community is focused on the most recent, negative data point (Q3 margin collapse) and is projecting this operational trough indefinitely into the future. This is a cognitive error driven by fear. Our edge is the Analytical conviction that the low margins are a result of aggressively accelerating CapEx (good spend) and associated OpEx (e.g., pre-revenue engineer hiring) required to meet insatiable demand. Once deployment catches up, the inherent operating leverage will reverse the current margin trend.

3. Linchpins (2-3 Key Drivers) (CRWV)

Linchpin 1: Operating Leverage Recovery

Our view: Operating margins must return to double digits (15%+) by Q2 2026.

Market view: Margins remain compressed <10% through 2026 due to competitive pricing.

Rationale: The specialized nature of CoreWeave's cloud (optimized for Generative AI workloads) commands a pricing premium that prevents full commoditization.

Linchpin 2: Debt/EBITDA Ratio Improvement

Our view: Forward EBITDA explodes as revenue hits $7.5B+ (FY26E), rapidly deleveraging the balance sheet from current high multiples to a manageable ratio of <4x within 18 months.

Risk: If deployment schedules slip, the ratio will remain stretched, creating solvency fears.

Rationale: The company's massive contracted revenue backlog underwrites the current debt.

Part 3: Iren Limited (IREN) - The Integrated Power Moat

Fair Value: $78 (prob-weighted) | Current: $47.68 | Upside: +63.6% | Conviction: Moderate

1. Thesis & Expectations Gap (IREN)

Iren Limited is a fundamentally mispriced vertically integrated power and data center asset. The market is still applying a discount based on its legacy Bitcoin mining business, failing to fully value the company's non-replicable asset base: secured, low-cost (as low as $0.035/kWh) renewable power capacity of 2.91 GW and its strategic pivot to AI Cloud Services, validated by the $9.7 billion Microsoft contract.

Market Expectation: At $47.68/share ($13.4B Market Cap, 30.3x P/E, highly volatile), the market prices in:

The persistent volatility and margin risk associated with the Bitcoin mining segment.

Significant skepticism regarding the speed and cost of converting 2GW of power capacity into high-performance AI compute, questioning the management's execution ability.

A "Conglomerate Discount" for operating two seemingly disparate businesses (Bitcoin and AI Cloud).

Our Variant View: The market is underestimating the structural cost moat afforded by IREN's integrated, low-cost power. Our focus is on the long-term Return on Invested Capital (ROIC). In a power-constrained AI world, IREN's low-cost power and vertical integration will translate into sustainably higher operating margins and FCF conversion rates for the AI Cloud segment compared to non-integrated peers. We forecast AI Cloud ARR reaching $600M+ by the end of FY26.

2. Source of Edge (BAIT) (IREN)

Primary Edge: Analytical (Power Moat and Optionality Value)

The value of secured, low-cost power at scale is severely understated. The analytical edge lies in valuing the asset as a utility-grade power supplier with a low-cost manufacturing operation (AI Cloud/Bitcoin) attached. This vertical integration allows IREN to generate FCF regardless of competitors' margins. The current high valuation is justified by the optionality value of the 2GW power capacity which can be converted from low-margin Bitcoin mining to high-margin AI Cloud, acting as an internal capital allocation mechanism.

3. Linchpins (2-3 Key Drivers) (IREN)

Linchpin 1: Sustained Low Power Cost (Cost of Revenue Moat)

Our view: Average long-term power costs remain stable below $0.04/kWh across the Childress and Sweetwater hubs, maintaining a 50%+ gross margin advantage over non-integrated peers.

Market view: Utility volatility and grid costs will compress the long-term cost advantage.

Rationale: Vertical integration, long-term power purchase agreements (PPAs), and secured land bank provide structural insulation.

Linchpin 2: AI Cloud Services ARR Momentum

Our view: Achieve AI Cloud ARR of $600M+ by the end of FY26, backed by the Microsoft agreement and new deployments.

Market view: AI revenue growth struggles to gain momentum, remaining <$500M ARR and subjected to delays.

Rationale: The low-cost power is the most powerful sales tool to attract hyperscaler and enterprise customers.
